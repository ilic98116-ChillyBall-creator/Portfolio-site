Dealer's Choice !

You walk into your first day of class, 90 minutes to create a poster. What does it become? My approach embraces chaos: fun, messy, and visually inspired by the bold aesthetics of the 1980s. Drawing from pop culture influences like Madonna and Michael Jackson, I incorporated vibrant makeup trends, arcade visuals, and exaggerated beauty standards that defined the era. Beneath this colourful surface, the poster reflects deeper tensions within Western society at the time. The 1980s were marked by shifting beauty ideals and rising body image pressures, alongside major social and political challenges; such as the AIDS epidemic, the war on drugs, Cold War anxieties, economic inequality, and debates surrounding abortion rights and LGBTQ+ rights. Through this contrast, I wanted to reveal how polished appearances often masked more complex and uncomfortable realities. The “grey areas” within the design represent these heavier conversations issues that, despite their difficulty, sparked resistance, activism, and meaningful social change. This piece explores how rebellion, expression, and speaking out can challenge societal norms and ultimately contribute to progress.

GO FISH !

Welcome! I'm Ana and I LOVE narrative design. My skills adapt from coding fundementals to the presentation of buisness and creative design. My passion stemmed in highschool when I took IB art without the required credentials from grade 9 and 10; however, I had the confidence in myself to display my efforts in sketching, sculpting and animation. Later in my academic career I was introduced to coding where my passion for gaming since childhood became a digital reality between my finger tips. Beyond that I discovered the arts of film and photography and developed a keen sense for close-up photography and landscape admiring the beauty of both architecture and natural beauty. And finally economics, previously in highschool I took IB econ as a mandatory course but in university when I encountered Andrew Gibson, he inspired me with a deep passion of how our society is constructed and developing throughout our presence from every willingness to pay as he once called it "girl math".

Brainstorm

My outdoor perspective was influence by one of my adored childhood films "Alice in Wonderland". The tales where psychedelic imagination becomes a childs playground full of twisted beings and environments that might cause a child to fright or freeze. In my world the crops can talk, the pumpkins can play, and the weather can start snowing with sugar cube residue falling from the sky. Perhaps the dream is from a foodie, or maybe they despise vegetables but in world as uncertain a "Tea Timeville"the world is scrambled with unsolved mysteries. the drawing istelf was a challenge as curved objects like msuhrooms and teapots are moreso avoided in perspective, more complexity and time to imagine their phsyical atmosphere. Nevertheless, in a world such as Tea Timeville the world can be as unproportional and dysfigured as much as it likes. It's when perfection kills the ignorance and becomes an inescapable reality.

Critique

In my indoor perspective I applied a silly twist as not often you see cartoons or storyboards at the morgues, usually people find them unsettling but in one of my ideas of short-films I wanted to reveal a small morgue character that doesn't neccessarily understand the concept of death as he is...wide awake and full of curiousity around such a delicate facility. His name is Tim, Tim as you can imagine in this scene is about to scare one of the poor older fellas that have been employed at the morgue for years, perfecting his craft t'ill he sees what science taught him to be the imaginable throughout his entire carrer, a second chance at life. Creating this enviornment took just as much time as the intricate details of medical tooks and storage cabins for all the personnas that lurk on the sidelines deserved just as much attention as the main plot. If I had the oppourtunity to make film I would let all the background characters have a story of their own to tell even in the after life.
