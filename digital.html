Landscape Photography

This collection explores outdoor spaces, urban scenery, and environmental composition through framing, light, and perspective. In this collection were my first sucessful photos of learning how to use a camera, from adujusting the lens, measuring the amount of light that hits the lens, to implementing a satisfactory composition.

Close-up Photography

These close-up works focus on texture, detail, and depth, capturing small subjects in a way that feels more dramatic and intimate. As much as these photos obtained a crisp result, the backstage work took multiple trial and error with an additional challenge of combating awkward social intercations like lying down on the train tracks, taking my shoes off in the center of the University campus during peak hour, and confusing peers at the library with my photos of books instead of reading.

Sport Photography

These images document motion, energy, and live action while focusing on timing, expression, and dynamic composition. Consistent trial and error of motion was key to catching such photos including increasing the framerate caught such dynamic shots. Lighting was also a challenge as the day had inconsistent lighting and weather from indoor change in lighting and outdoor whether going from sunny to cloudly reptitivley.

Profile Photography

This set highlights personality and presence through portraits that emphasize posture, mood, and visual storytelling. The selfies I took captured the raw emotions of my first trials with photography as I try to grasp and balance the visions in my head to my present capibilities of what skills I had as a beginner photographer. Instead of perfection I embarced the imperfection that people don't see as often for example angled shots, pixel grid lens and moody colors.

Freelance Photography

This freelance work reflects client-based projects where composition, subject focus, and adaptability were important to the final result. The context of these images were a client suggested that two of his partnering collegues would travel from Quebec to see the landscape and livley enviornment of Waterloo campus as a day trip vlogging their experiance. Their adventures ranged from recordings at the Funken Cafe to discussing challenges what young liberals can combat while seeing future leaders at E7.
